Project 2: Parking Spots Image Classifier

Image Classifier Project using TensorFlow and Keras Description: This project utilizes TensorFlow and Keras to create a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for binary image classification. The goal is to differentiate between two classes: empty and occupied spaces. The dataset is split into training and test sets, incorporating data augmentation during training for enhanced generalization. The CNN architecture includes convolutional layers, max-pooling layers, and dense layers. The model is compiled with binary crossentropy loss and the Adam optimizer. Training progress, accuracy, and loss metrics are visually presented using matplotlib. The final model achieves an impressive test accuracy of approximately 93.93%. This project serves as a showcase of deep learning applications in image classification, specifically for space occupancy detection.

Tools Used: TensorFlow Keras Matplotlib

Achievements: Successfully implemented a CNN for binary image classification. Demonstrated the effectiveness of data augmentation for model generalization. Attained a commendable test accuracy of 93.93%.

Research

Predictive Analytics for Opioid Crisis Management: A Machine Learning Approach

**Description:** Our collaborative research project with Prof. Mike Hughes at Tufts focuses on leveraging machine learning algorithms to predict spatiotemporal counts of opioid-related overdoses. This initiative aims to provide healthcare experts with advanced tools for handling future occurrences, particularly in regions with sparse historical overdose data.

**Objective:** The primary objective is to enhance the predictive accuracy of opioid overdose occurrences, allowing for more proactive and targeted healthcare interventions. By combining machine learning algorithms and sophisticated models, we seek to contribute to the development of effective strategies in combating the opioid crisis.

**Methodology:** Our approach involves the implementation of state-of-the-art Zero-Inflated Gaussian Process and Zero-Inflated Poisson likelihood models. These models address the challenges posed by sparsely distributed historical overdose data, enabling prioritization of regions in urgent need of healthcare resources. Additionally, extensive analysis of historical overdose data for Massachusetts forms a crucial component of our methodology.

**Impact:** The potential impact of this research is significant, offering healthcare professionals advanced predictive tools to anticipate and mitigate opioid-related overdoses effectively. This proactive approach can lead to more targeted resource allocation, potentially saving lives and reducing the overall impact of the opioid crisis.

**Future Directions:** Moving forward, our research will focus on refining and expanding our models. We aim to explore additional data sources to further improve predictive capabilities. Additionally, we envision integrating emerging technologies and methodologies to stay at the forefront of predictive analytics in healthcare.
